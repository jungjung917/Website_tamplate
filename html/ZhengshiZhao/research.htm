Main Project 1: Blue straggler stars

  • Blue straggler stars
  • Photometric study of BBS in dSphs
  • Photometric study of BBS in GCs
  • Spectroscopic study of BSS in OCs
  • Publication and Presentations
Blue straggler stars (BSSs) lie above the current main sequence (MS) turnoff (TO) on the color-magnitude diagram (CMD) of star cluster. Their potions on the CMD imply that they are more massive than current MSTO stars in the same cluster. Thus, their presense of the MS was a challenge for standard stellar evolution thoery, as normal stars with the same mass have already evolved into white dwarfs. To explain the extraordinary long lifespan on the MS, various mechanisms have been suggested, all involving in additional hydrogen material input to normal stars. Two of the most popular mechanisms are merger by direct collisions (CL) in single stars and coalescence or mass transfer (MT) in close binary systems.

As products of dynamical processes in the host cluster and stellar evolution itself, studying BSSs can shed a light on the process of merger and/or mass transfer, and be a tracer of the dynamical evolution of the host cluster. However, we are still faced with problems in understanding these objects clearly and completely. The aim of my research is to derive a comprehensive interpretation for origins of BSS by investigating the BSS properties in different star systems, such as OCs, GCs and dSphs based on observational data of the highest quality up to the present day.
CMD of Globular cluster M3. ( Ferraro et al. 1997)

We have collected homogeneous deep photometric data for 9 dSphs taken from Suprime-Cam/Subaru Telescope. The sample includes 3 classical dSphs, Draco, Ursa Minor (UMi) and Sextans and 6 ultra faint dwarf galaxies (UFDs). For the 3 classical dSphs, we have analyzed the BSSs’ radial distributions and luminosity functions (LF). In these 3 galaxies, BSSs radial distribution shows a lack of strong central peak and LF is independent of radius. These results imply that in these dSphs, the blue plume stars are most likely real BSSs formed mainly by mass transfer in primordial binaries. The frequency of BSSs in the 9 galaxies does not correlate with galaxy parameters, such as half light radius, metallicity, central surface brightness, concentration parameter, central density, velocity dispersion, half mass relaxation time and collisional rate, as well as integrated absolute magnitude. This result suggests that the evolution of primordial binaries (PBs), which would form BSSs, was not affected significantly by dynamical interactions in such sparse environments. Thus, the observed BSS frequency is almost the same in these galaxies under the simple hypothesis that the PB fraction was the same in all galaxy.

We have collected homogeneous deep photometric data for 51 GC obtained by ACS/HST. 8 GCs in our sample show bimodal BSSs frequency radial distributions. Among them, the bimodal distribution is reported for the first time in NGC 5286, NGC 5986, NGC 6362, NGC 6535, NGC6541 and NGC7099, in total 6 GCs. It has raised the number of this kind of GCs by twice the number known before. Moreover, the significant anti-correlation between BSS frequency and Mv is confirmed with our sample. BSS frequency is also significantly anti-correlated to central density and central velocity dispersion. Furthermore, LFs of BSSs in bright (Mv < -8.8) and faint (Mv > -8.8) clusters do not show significant differences. One possible interpretation for all these properties is that BSSs formation via collisions in GCs, at least for GCs of comparable ages to ours, is not the dominant mechanism. Thus, BSS’ LFs do not vary with brightness. On the contrary, collisions destroyed PBs and/or accelerated the evolution of PBs in the past, since this is more effective in the brighter (more massive) clusters, decreased numbers of BSSs at present lead to the anti-correlation of BSS frequency and Mv, central density as well as central velocity dispersion.

We have observed 30 BSSs and 9 MSTOs candidates in three old Galactic OCs, NGC 2682, NGC 2112 and NGC 6819 by using HDS/Subaru Telescope. Among them, radial velocities of all target stars in NGC 2682 and NGC 6819, and 6 BSSs and all TOs in NGC 2112 are consistent with mean radial velocities of member stars in the clusters and thus we considered these stars are real cluster members. Rotation velocity of BSSs spans a wide range from few kms−1 to hundreds kms−1, while those of TOs vary in small range from few kms−1 to 25 kms−1. The high rotation velocity of current BSS is consistendt with expectation of mass transfer formation mechanism. Both some of BSSs and TOs show Li depletion while the others show normal Li abundances. Interestingly, BS1371 in NGC 2112 and BS 10010 in NGC 6819, show very high (> 3.5) Li abundances. One BSS in NGC 2682 and two BSSs in NGC6189 are enhanced in O abundance, while depletion in C abundance is common in BSSs in NGC 2112 and NGC 6819. These anomalies in light elements could be interpreted by mass transfer mechanism.

Main Project 2: Solar twins and Solar Analogue stars

  • Spectroscopic analysis of solar analogues
  • Publication and Presentations

High resolution and high signal to noise ratio spectra of 27 photometrically-selected solar analogs were obtained by using 2.16m telescope of Xinglong station of NAOC. We carried out a search for solar twin candidates based on the comparisons of atmospheric parameters, detailed spectrum match and spectral synthesis of lines. Based on our results, we found that HD164595 is one of the closest solar twin star known to date. We have also derived accurate abundances of nearly 10 elements with these spectra by using the LTE models. [O/Fe] shows a tendency　to decrease with increasing metalicity for -0.3<[Fe/H]<0.3. Oxygen is very sensitive to different temperatures in stars with Teff<5700K. [O/Fe] decreases　with increasing Teff, but the distribution of [O/Fe] shows no significant trend for Teff>5700K. Alpha elements (like Si, Ca and Ti) show no clear tendency with increasing metalicity. In agreement with most works, the slope of [X/Fe] of Fe-peak elements (like Cr and Ni) seems to be flat. In particular, solar abundances do not show any abnormalities based on our results.

Cooperate Project: Chemical composition of giants from two moving groups
My contribution for this project was participation of preparing observation proposal and the observation.

  • Chemical composition of giants from two moving groups
  • Publication and Presentations
We present the stellar parameters of 19 K-type giants and their abundances for 13 chemical elements (Al, Ba, Ca, Fe, K, Mg, Mn, Na, Ni, Sc, Si, Ti and V), selected from two moving groups, covering the metallicity range of -0.6 < [Fe/H] < 0.2, based on high-resolution spectra. Most of the elemental abundances show similar trends as in previous studies, except for Al, Na and Ba, which are seriously affected by evolution. The abundance ratios of [Na/Mg] increase smoothly with higher [Mg/H], and those of [Al/Mg] decrease slightly with increasing [Mg/H]. The abundance ratios of [Mg/Ba] show a distinction between these two moving groups, which is mainly induced by chemical evolution and also partly by kinematic effects. The inhomogeneous metallicity of each star from the moving groups demonstrates that these stars had different chemical origins before they were kinematically aggregated. This favours a dynamical resonant theory.
